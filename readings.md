# Reading List

## Core readings

1. Lemercier, Claire, and Claire Zalc. 2019. *Quantitative Methods in the Humanities: An Introduction*. Charlottesville: University of Virginia Press. Chapter 6, "Visualizing History." <https://doi.org/10.2307/j.ctvbqs963.10>.
2. Barabasi, Albert-Laszlo. 2016. *Network Science Book*. Chapter 1. <https://networksciencebook.com/chapter/1>.

## Extended list

- Algee-Hewitt, Mark. 2017. "Distributed Character: Quantitative Models of the English Stage, 1550-1900." *New Literary History* 48 (4): 751-782. <https://doi.org/10.1353/nlh.2017.0038>. *(Drama analysis)*
- Ahnert, Ruth, and Sebastian E. Ahnert. 2019. "Metadata, Surveillance and the Tudor State." *History Workshop Journal*. *(History)* Additional website: <https://tudornetworks.net/>.
- Eder, Maciej. 2017. "Visualization in Stylometry: Cluster Analysis Using Networks." *Digital Scholarship in the Humanities* 32 (1): 50-64. *(Stylometry, Linguistic)*
- Granovetter, Mark S. 1973. "The Strength of Weak Ties." *American Journal of Sociology* 78 (6): 1360-1380. *(Sociology, foundational paper)*
- Ochab, Jeremi K., Jan Skvrnak, and Michael Skvrnak. 2022. "Detecting Ottokar II's 1248-1249 Uprising and Its Instigators in Co-Witnessing Networks." *Historical Methods: A Journal of Quantitative and Interdisciplinary History* 55 (4): 189-208. <https://doi.org/10.1080/01615440.2022.2065397>. *(History)*
- Romanello, Matteo. n.d. "Exploring Intertextuality in Classics through Citation Networks." *Digital Humanities Quarterly* 10 (2). *(Science of Science)*
- Stutzer, Reto, Adrian Rinscheid, T. D. Oliveira, et al. 2021. "Black Coal, Thin Ice: The Discursive Legitimisation of Australian Coal in the Age of Climate Change." *Humanities and Social Sciences Communications* 8: 178. <https://doi.org/10.1057/s41599-021-00827-5>. *(Environmental Studies)*
- Szemes, Botond, and Mihaly Nagy. 2025. "Temporal Aspects of Structural Differences in Dramatic Genre." *Zeitschrift fur digitale Geisteswissenschaften* 10. Published June 5, 2025. <https://doi.org/10.17175/2025_007>. *(Drama analysis)*
- Watts, Duncan J., and Steven H. Strogatz. 1998. "Collective Dynamics of 'Small-World' Networks." *Nature* 393: 440-442. <https://doi.org/10.1038/30918>. *(Sociology, foundational paper)*
